Our Values & Objectives

Not aspirations on a wall. Operating principles embedded into how we work.

CATS Insights operates according to five core objectives. They are not aspirational statements. They are structural commitments, each one embedded into the way the organisation trains its members, selects its leaders, reviews its articles, and plans its future.

01

Academic Excellence and Intellectual Curiosity

To publish high quality, peer reviewed student research and articles that demonstrates rigorous academic scholarship that meets university-level academic standards. Contributors must produce work they themselves would read and learn from, rather than merely submitting unsubstantive essays for impressive sounding credentials for university applications. Quality assurance protocols will ensure scholarly integrity while preserving student voice and creative ideas.

02

Accessible Student Leadership & Skill Development Opportunity

To provide all interested students with genuine opportunities to develop research, writing, editorial, organisation, leadership and project management skills regardless of prior experience or academic standing. This objective democratises super-curricular engagement while offering invaluable leadership roles with real decision-making authority and responsibilities that will cultivate and foster transferable skills for academic and professional life. Leadership opportunities will be designated for mentorship and collaborative growth rather than aggrandisement.

03

Intellectual Exploration & Interest Discovery Platform

To empower students to pursue STEM topics or fields of academic study that genuinely fascinate and interest them and this includes areas beyond the classroom syllabus or examination requirements. This objective seeks to nurture curiosity driven learning which will be free from examination grade pressure to encourage exploration of recent research, emerging technologies and interdisciplinary learning on foreign and new academic fields which students might not be familiar on. My goal is intrinsic intellectual proactivity, to learn new things for the joy of it rather than staying in one’s comfort zone and studying only in the most basic scope like it is a menial chore.

04

Legacy & Sustainable Impact

To develop institutional frameworks, documentation and organisation systems that ensure CATS Insights benefits students long after I graduate. This is based on my commitment to collective impact for my fellow students rather than for my own individual gain. Through long-term thinking, my goal is to embed comprehensive operational manuals, tutorials and templates, lessons and protocols into the constitution of CATS Insights and its student culture. This will ensure that future generations can continue to benefit from my endeavour sustainably. I wish to build something that will endure and matter beyond my tenure.

"Legacy, what is a legacy? It's planting seeds in a garden you never get to see." — Lin-Manuel Miranda
05

Community Impact & Social Responsibility

To extend STEM and academic access to underprivileged students and peoples through structured charitable initiatives. These could include virtual workshops or hands-on workshops inspired by published articles, mentorship programmes and the development and donation of accessible educational resources to schools and community centres. It is my conviction that intellectual achievement and social responsibility go hand in hand. Scholarship cannot exist for just one’s benefit but must rather cause tangible societal good. Once operational stability is achieved, CATS Insights will propose further charitable initiatives sustainably to create meaningful action and impact.

Who Leads: Selection Criteria

The organisation selects its leaders based on observed performance and verifiable commitment.

  1. Reliability — Consistent attendance, punctuality, and follow-through on commitments.
  2. Academic Strength — Genuine engagement with subjects and capacity for intellectual growth.
  3. Time Management — Ability to sustain productivity under competing academic pressures.
  4. Collaboration — Clear communication, emotional intelligence, and willingness to adapt.
  5. Initiative — Proactive problem-solving and accountability for both success and failure.
