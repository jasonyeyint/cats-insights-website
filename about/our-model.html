Our Model & How We Work

Structure, process, and the systems that make it function.

Organisational Architecture

The organisation operates on a three-tier hierarchy to prevent decision paralysis, ensure accountability, and give every member clarity on where responsibility lies.

Tier 1 — Strategic

  • President: Kyal Sin Ye Yint (Jason)
  • Vice President: Alex

Responsibility: Direction, editorial vision, faculty relationships, final approval authority. Prevents decision paralysis.

Tier 2 — Operational

The Captains. Managing departments, mentorship, quality control, and week-to-week momentum. Implementation of the core strategy.

Tier 3 — Execution

~22+ General members conducting the intellectual work: Writing, Editing, Designing, and Outreach activities.

The Publication Workflow

Stage 1 — Planning (2 weeks)

Topic selection, recruitment, and theme establishment. Led by President and Captains.

Stage 2 — Writing & Learning (6–8 weeks)

Drafting with mentorship, workshops, and iterative feedback loops. Solo: 500-1000 words. Adv: 2000+.

Stage 3 — Editorial Processing (3–4 weeks)

Peer review, revisions, Turnitin <20% similarity check, and strict manual authorship verification via Google Docs version history and editorial judgement.

Stage 4 — Design & Layout (2–3 weeks)

Designers carefully format the articles to be released in the official publication.

Stage 5 — Final Review (1–2 weeks)

Faculty advisor (Miss Emma) audit and optional external expert consultation for final approval.

Stage 6 — Publication & Launch (1 week)

Digital release, social media distribution, and email broadcast across all 7 CATS Global Schools.

Full cycle: approximately 5–6 months per edition.

Quality Standards

Test Critical Question Required Standard
Specialist Test Would an expert find errors? Zero scientific inaccuracies.
Peer Test Can an A-Level student follow? Accessible but rigorous.
Citation Test Is it credible & traceable? Must follow our detailed citation protocol.
