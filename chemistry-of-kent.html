Philosophy

What exactly are ideas?

By Luca Candelot  |  8 min read

What is an idea, really? This article traces the concept of the "Idea" from Kant's transcendental dialectics through to Gilles Deleuze's radical reconceptualisation in Difference and Repetition — arguing that ideas are not essences but multiplicities: complex, open-ended problems that persist regardless of whether they have a solution. A lucid introduction to one of contemporary philosophy's most demanding concepts.

Superconductivity: Materials and Underlying Mechanisms

By Oleksandr Boyko (Alex)  |  9 min read

Certain materials, when cooled below a critical temperature, conduct electricity with absolutely zero resistance. This article by CATS Insights Vice President Oleksandr Boyko unpacks the quantum mechanical basis of superconductivity — from Cooper pair formation to the Meissner effect — and traces the technology from its accidental 1911 discovery to its real-world applications in MRI scanners, maglev trains, and particle accelerators.
