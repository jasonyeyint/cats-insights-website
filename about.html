About CATS Insights

The story behind a student-built institution.

CATS Insights was born from a gap. Students at The Worthgate School had limited supercurricular and extracurricular opportunities to develop the skills that matter most beyond the classroom: academic writing, independent research, editorial judgement, design thinking, and organisational leadership. There was no platform where a student could pursue a question that genuinely fascinated them, work on it with real rigour, and produce something worth reading at the end. No structured pathway to build a portfolio, strengthen a university application, or discover whether research and writing were skills they wanted to carry into their future.

So rather than wait for one to appear, we built it from scratch.

CATS Insights is a student-run academic publication, founded and operated entirely by students. Every role carries real responsibility. Writers research and draft original articles on topics they choose. Editors provide structured feedback that teaches contributors to think more clearly and argue more precisely. Designers translate finished work into a publication that looks as serious as the scholarship inside it. And the entire operation runs on a set of frameworks, guides, and training programmes developed specifically to ensure that no student needs prior experience to contribute, only curiosity, determination and a willingness to learn.

Our Core Objectives

The organisation is governed by five core objectives that inform every decision we make:

Academic excellence and intellectual curiosity

We publish work that meets a standard our contributors would genuinely want to read, not essays manufactured for a credential.

Accessible leadership and skill development

Every student, regardless of experience or academic standing, has a genuine opportunity to develop real skills through real work.

Intellectual exploration and interest discovery

Our members pursue topics beyond the syllabus, free from examination pressure. The goal is intrinsic curiosity, learning something new for the joy of understanding it, not because it will be tested.

Legacy and sustainable impact

Everything we build is documented, systematised, and designed to outlast the people who created it. Future students inherit a functioning institution, not a memory of one.

Community impact and social responsibility

Intellectual achievement and social responsibility are inseparable. As CATS Insights matures, we intend to extend academic access to underprivileged students through workshops, mentorship, and freely available educational resources.

These are not aspirations written on a wall. They are operating principles embedded into how the organisation trains its members, reviews its work, and measures its success. The publication is the visible output. The development of the students who make it is the purpose.

Organisational Structure & Governance

Our robust operational framework ensures sustainability beyond a single cohort. We operate on a 3-tier system:

1. Strategic Tier

The Senior Editorial Board establishes wide-reaching policies, sets the publication agenda, and handles quality assurance pipelines.

2. Operational Tier

Lead Writers and Section Editors manage specific topics, act as mentors to Junior Writers, and ensure consistent tone and accuracy.

3. Execution Tier

Writers and Researchers draft content, conduct peer reviews, and source accurate chemistry literature.

Governance

Our operations are standardised across 48 documents organised into 7 distinct suites (e.g., Editorial Guidelines, Onboarding, External Communications). This deep institutional knowledge prevents skill loss when senior members graduate.

Quality Assurance Pipeline

Every piece of evidence and article goes through our strict 4-stage audit to guarantee academic integrity:

  • Stage 1: Peer Review (Content & Chemistry Accuracy)
  • Stage 2: Senior Editorial Review (Style & Formatting)
  • Stage 3: Plagiarism & AI Checks (Turnitin <15% threshold, GPTZero scan)
  • Stage 4: Citation Audit (Oxford Referencing standard validation)

Succession Planning

Longevity is built into our core framework:

  • The Deputy System: Every key leadership role must have a trained deputy.
  • The Succession Test: Upon graduation, knowledge transfer must be complete enough that incoming leaders can immediately take over without disruption.
