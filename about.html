About CATS Insights

A student-built institution. An academic publication with a mission.

"We know what we are, but know not what we may be." — William Shakespeare

CATS Insights was born from a gap. Students at The Worthgate School had limited supercurricular and extracurricular opportunities to develop the skills that matter most beyond the classroom: academic writing, independent research, editorial judgement, design thinking, and organisational leadership. There was no platform where a student could pursue a question that genuinely fascinated them, work on it with real rigour, and produce something worth reading at the end. No structured pathway to build a portfolio, strengthen a university application, or discover whether research and writing were skills they wanted to carry into their future.

So rather than wait for one to appear, we built it from scratch.

CATS Insights is a student-run academic publication, founded and operated entirely by students. Every role carries real responsibility. Writers research and draft original articles on topics they choose. Editors provide structured feedback that teaches contributors to think more clearly and argue more precisely. Designers translate finished work into a publication that looks as serious as the scholarship inside it. And the entire operation runs on a set of frameworks, guides, and training programmes developed specifically to ensure that no student needs prior experience to contribute, only curiosity, determination and a willingness to learn.

Our Core Objectives

The organisation is governed by five core objectives that inform every decision we make:

Academic Excellence and Intellectual Curiosity

To publish high quality, peer reviewed student research and articles that demonstrates rigorous academic scholarship that meets university-level academic standards. Contributors must produce work they themselves would read and learn from, rather than merely submitting unsubstantive essays for impressive sounding credentials for university applications. Quality assurance protocols will ensure scholarly integrity while preserving student voice and creative ideas.

Accessible Student Leadership & Skill Development Opportunity

To provide all interested students with genuine opportunities to develop research, writing, editorial, organisation, leadership and project management skills regardless of prior experience or academic standing. This objective democratises super-curricular engagement while offering invaluable leadership roles with real decision-making authority and responsibilities that will cultivate and foster transferable skills for academic and professional life. Leadership opportunities will be designated for mentorship and collaborative growth rather than aggrandisement.

Intellectual Exploration & Interest Discovery Platform

To empower students to pursue STEM topics or fields of academic study that genuinely fascinate and interest them and this includes areas beyond the classroom syllabus or examination requirements. This objective seeks to nurture curiosity driven learning which will be free from examination grade pressure to encourage exploration of recent research, emerging technologies and interdisciplinary learning on foreign and new academic fields which students might not be familiar on. My goal is intrinsic intellectual proactivity, to learn new things for the joy of it rather than staying in one’s comfort zone and studying only in the most basic scope like it is a menial chore.

Legacy & Sustainable Impact

To develop institutional frameworks, documentation and organisation systems that ensure CATS Insights benefits students long after I graduate. This is based on my commitment to collective impact for my fellow students rather than for my own individual gain. Through long-term thinking, my goal is to embed comprehensive operational manuals, tutorials and templates, lessons and protocols into the constitution of CATS Insights and its student culture. This will ensure that future generations can continue to benefit from my endeavour sustainably. I wish to build something that will endure and matter beyond my tenure.

Community Impact & Social Responsibility

To extend STEM and academic access to underprivileged students and peoples through structured charitable initiatives. These could include virtual workshops or hands-on workshops inspired by published articles, mentorship programmes and the development and donation of accessible educational resources to schools and community centres. It is my conviction that intellectual achievement and social responsibility go hand in hand. Scholarship cannot exist for just one’s benefit but must rather cause tangible societal good. Once operational stability is achieved, CATS Insights will propose further charitable initiatives sustainably to create meaningful action and impact.

These are not aspirations written on a wall. They are operating principles embedded into how the organisation trains its members, reviews its work, and measures its success. The publication is the visible output. The development of the students who make it is the purpose.

The Editorial & Quality Assurance Pipeline

Every article in CATS Insights follows a fixed, non-negotiable multi-stage pipeline. No article reaches the design team without completing all editorial stages and clearing strict academic audits.

1. The Editorial Pipeline

  • Draft Submission: Initial structured drafts submitted by Writers.
  • Edit 1 (Structural Edit): Big-picture review focusing on argument logic, logical fallacies, and thesis clarity.
  • Writer Revision: Mandatory writer responses to structural feedback.
  • Edit 2 (Line Edit): Sentence-level review focusing on academic tone, clarity, and hedging language.
  • Lead Edit: Final editorial review for cross-publication consistency.

2. Quality Assurance Protocols

Following Lead Edit sign-off, articles enter the QA Gate to guarantee academic integrity:

  • Plagiarism Audit: Submissions must pass Turnitin similarity checks (strict <20% threshold).
  • Authorship Verification: Due to the unreliability of automated AI detectors, we rely on rigorous manual editorial judgement and require verifiable proof of human drafting (e.g., Google Docs version history) rather than trusting automated scans.
  • Citation Validation: Verification of Tier 1/Tier 2 source credibility and precise formatting.
  • Ready for Design (RFD): Only upon passing all audits is an article cleared for the visual design phase.

Social Impact & Model Citizenship

CATS Insights is committed to being more than just an academic publication; we aim to produce students who are model citizens. This commitment is evidenced by our ongoing engagement with school events and charitable initiatives.

Charity & School Impact

Through our digital outreach and school presence, we have coordinated and supported multiple initiatives that raise money for very good causes. Our members are encouraged to see their academic passion as a tool for public good, using the visibility of CATS Insights to raise awareness for social issues and support charitable fundraising efforts within The Worthgate School community.

Succession & Institutional Legacy

A core challenge for any student-run organisation is the "cycle of graduation." To prevent CATS Insights from disappearing when its founders leave, we have institutionalised every piece of knowledge required to run it.

The Succession Test

"If every current member left tomorrow, could a new team publish Edition 1 within 6 months using only our documents?" This is the standard to which our 48+ core foundational documents are held, ensuring the organisation's "DNA" is preserved.

The Deputy System

Our tiered leadership structure ensures that every leader has a trained successor ready to step into their role. This approach prevents leadership voids and ensures professional continuity across academic years.
